# Liver, Beef, and Caramelized Onions with Mushrooms

This hearty dish combines rich flavors and textures for a satisfying, nutrient-dense meal. The sweetness of caramelized onions balances the liver beautifully.

## Ingredients

### Vegetables

* 3 large onions, thinly sliced
* 1 lb mushrooms, sliced
* 3 cloves garlic, minced

### Protein

* 1.5 lbs beef liver
* 1 lb ground beef (93% lean or leaner)

### Seasonings & Other

* Cooking spray
* 1-2 tablespoons water or beef broth (for cooking)
* 1 cup whole milk (for soaking liver) - discarded, no calories retained
* 1 teaspoon salt (plus more to taste)
* 3/4 teaspoon black pepper (divided)
* 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
* 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
* 1 teaspoon dried parsley (optional)
* Soy sauce (low-sodium if preferred), for serving
* Couscous, rice, or roasted yams, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the liver:** Slice the liver into 1/2-inch thick pieces and soak in milk for 20-30 minutes (this reduces any bitterness). Drain and pat very dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper.

2. **Caramelize the onions:** Spray a large nonstick skillet with cooking spray and heat over medium heat. Add the sliced onions with a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 25-30 minutes until deeply golden and caramelized. Add a tablespoon of water whenever they start to stick (this replaces oil/butter). Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

3. **Cook the mushrooms:** Spray the same skillet with cooking spray over medium-high heat. Add the mushrooms and cook without stirring for 3-4 minutes to get a nice sear, then stir and continue cooking until golden and their liquid has evaporated, about 8-10 minutes total. Add the garlic and cook 1 minute more. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to the bowl with the onion mixture.

4. **Cook the ground beef:** In the same skillet over medium-high heat (no spray needed if using lean beef), brown the ground beef, breaking it apart as it cooks. Season with 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, the smoked paprika, and thyme. Cook until browned and cooked through, about 8 minutes. Drain any excess fat. Transfer to the bowl with the vegetables.

5. **Cook the liver:** Wipe out the skillet and spray lightly with cooking spray over medium heat (don't go too hot—liver becomes tough when overcooked). Working in batches if needed, cook the liver pieces for 2-3 minutes per side until browned outside but still slightly pink in the center. Don't overcook or it will become grainy. Remove from heat and let rest for 2 minutes, then chop into small bite-sized pieces.

6. **Combine everything:** Return all components to the skillet—the caramelized onions, mushrooms, ground beef, and chopped liver. Toss gently over low heat for 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through. Add a splash of water or broth if it seems dry. Taste and adjust seasoning. Sprinkle with dried parsley if using.

7. **Serve:** Plate over couscous or rice, or alongside a roasted yam. Pass soy sauce at the table for everyone to add to taste.

## Notes

- Use 95% lean ground beef to minimize fat
- Cooking spray instead of oil/butter saves about 300+ calories
- Water or broth for deglazing adds moisture without calories
- Liver is naturally very lean and nutrient-dense
- The onions and mushrooms add volume and nutrients while keeping calories low
- Serve with cauliflower rice instead of regular rice to cut calories further
- Roast yams without oil by wrapping in foil

**Serves:** 4-6
